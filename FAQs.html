FAQs

Why should I submit my article? -- Its already published somehwere else!

Additional exposure for your great work. Contribution to coordinated effort to advance ecological forecasting research. Its really easy to submit: Send an email to containing a link to the published article (no other information or action is required).

Why not publish our own?

We want to be able to publish the very best articles, and these should appear in Nature, Science, PNAS, Ecology Letters etc. We don't want to contribute to articles about ecological forecasting not being published in other great journals. With our publishing model, we, and authors, can have our cake and eat it.

Are we parasitising others work

One could take that perspective. We prefer to think that we add new value to already published articles and provide a subject specific venue for articles published in very general locations, such as biorxiv and PLoSOne.

Are articles peer reviewed?

Yes, if the article is originally published in a venue that conducts peer review. No if not.

Why must submitted articles have a DOI?

Because articles with a DOI have greater permanence than those that don't.

Editors

Anyone can participate in consideration and discussion of articles, thus anyone can be an Editor. A present, the discussions will happen on Github, and therefore require editors to have a Github account.

Is this an overlay journal.

Overlay journals seem to be defined as publishing only open access articles. We don't have such a limitation (though we're happier if articles are open access).

I have a suggestion about how the journal can be improved.

Such as not including this in the FAQs, as its not a question! Please send suggestions to , or get on Github, where the journal is hosted, and submit your suggestion as an Issue.
